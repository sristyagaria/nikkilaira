PROTIENS

Protein is a macronutrient that is essential to building muscle mass. It is commonly found in animal products, though is also present in other sources, such as nuts and legumes. There are three macronutrients: protein, fats and carbohydrates. Macronutrients provide calories, or energy

HERE IS SOME INFORMATION ABOUT PROTIENS:

  • INFORMATION VIDEO
  • STRUCTURE

    • Protein structure is the three-dimensional arrangement of atoms in an amino acid-chain molecule. Proteins are polymers – specifically polypeptides – formed from sequences of amino acids, the monomers of the polymer. ... Protein structures range in size from tens to several thousand amino acids.

  • NUTRITION

    • Protein is essential for growth and repair of the body and maintenance of good health. Protein also provides energy; 1 gram provides 17 kJ (4 kcal). The Reference Nutrient Intake (RNI) is set at 0.75g of protein per kilogram bodyweight per day for adults
